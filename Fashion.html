Mock Neck Sweater Weather



WINTER IS COMING! If you read GoT you know what I'm talking about. If you don't, here's a quick synopsis: it's f*cking freezing and I can't hang. Hence the mock neck sweater. As for the ripped jeans, I already heard it from my grandmother as I left the house, but honestly they look cool and cold knees are a price I am willing to pay for that...

This outfit plays on my go-to monochromatic style when I'm pressed for time in the mornings. And let's be real, I'm always pressed for time in the mornings. Usually it's all black, but I thought it would be fun to switch it up with whites and beige. Yeah, beige, so fun. But if you could feel as cozy as I did in this Sanctuary clothing sweater then you would realize just how fun it was. I'm usually skeptical of wool sweaters because they tend to be itchy, but not this one. It is perfectly warm and fuzzy which comes in clutch at work considering my desk is right under the air conditioning vent. Air conditioning? In winter?? I know right!?!? Don't worry, I'm writing a ticket.

But back to the point, here's how I styled my beige mock neck sweater:

As I mentioned above, I wanted to keep the color palette neutral. I love mixing different shades of the same color family because I think it allows the details of the clothes to be the focal point of the outfit. In this case, I wanted the focus to be on the mock neck sweater. This sweater from Sanctuary has interesting stich patterns and the subtle high low hemline with mini side slits make it different from any other knit I own. I paired it with cropped, slightly distressed jeans to lighten up the heavy look that oversize sweaters can create. I finished it off with a pair of suede ankle boots with a wooden heel to add in more texture and interest to the overall look.
